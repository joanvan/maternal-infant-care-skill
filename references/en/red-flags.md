# Red Flags (Maternal & Infant)

## Maternal urgent red flags
- Heavy vaginal bleeding pattern
- Severe persistent abdominal/chest pain, dyspnea, syncope
- Seizure, severe headache with visual changes
- Fever with foul lochia or severe breast inflammation symptoms
- Unilateral leg swelling/pain with breathing symptoms
- Self-harm or harm-to-baby thoughts

## Infant urgent red flags
- Under 3 months: fever >=38.0 C
- Respiratory distress (retractions, grunting, cyanosis)
- Persistent poor feeding + low urine output
- Repeated forceful/bile-stained vomiting, blood in stool
- Seizure, unresponsiveness, rapidly worsening jaundice with poor feeding

## Yellow tier (same day/24h review)
- Persistent moderate fever, worsening cough/diarrhea/rash, feeding decline
